Absence of Odd-Parity Bound States in a Shallow Square Well

Source: D. Tong, Quantum Mechanics, Problem Sheet 1, Question 7.

Consider a square well potential with $U(x) = -U$ for $|x| < a$ and $U(x) = 0$ otherwise ($U$ is a positive constant). Show that there are no bound states (normalisable energy eigenfunctions) which satisfy $\chi(-x) = -\chi(x)$ (i.e.\ which have odd parity) if

\[a^2 U < \frac{(\pi\hbar)^2}{8m}\,. \tag{1.1}\]

For the state to be bounded, we require $-U<E<0$. Solving the TISE, we find \[\chi(x) = \begin{cases} Ae^{\kappa x} & x\leq-a,\\ C\sin(kx)+ D\cos(kx) & |x|<a,\\ Ee^{-\kappa x} & x\geq a, \end{cases}\tag{1.2}\] with \[\kappa = \frac{\sqrt{-2mE}}{\hbar} \qquad \text{and} \qquad k = \frac{\sqrt{2m(U+E)}}{\hbar}.\tag{1.3}\]

For the wavefunction to be odd, we require $-A=E$ and $D=0$. Imposing the boundary conditions, we find \[\frac{\tan ka}{ka} = -\frac{1}{\kappa},\tag{1.4}\] which is an implicit transcendental equation for $E$. We can rewrite the equation as \[k\cot ka = -\kappa(k),\tag{1.5}\] where \[\kappa^2 + k^2 = \frac{2mU}{\hbar^2}.\tag{1.6}\]

Since $\cot$ has a greater magnitude of gradient than a circle; for a solution to exist, we require the radius of the circle to be greater than $ka=\pi/2$. That is, \[\frac{\sqrt{2mU}}{\hbar} > \frac{\pi}{2a} \implies Ua^2 > \frac{\pi^2\hbar^2}{8m}.\tag{1.7}\]
