Relativistic Motion in a Uniform Electric Field

Source: D. Tong, Electromagnetism, Problem Sheet 3, Question 9.

A particle of rest mass $m$ and charge $q$ moves in a constant uniform electric field $\mathbf{E} = (E,0,0)$. It starts from the origin with initial momentum $\mathbf{p} = (0,p_0,0)$. Show that the particle traces out a path in the $(x,y)$ plane given by \[x = \frac{\mathcal{E}_0}{qE}\left(\cosh\!\left(\frac{qEy}{p_0 c}\right)-1\right),\tag{1}\] where $\mathcal{E}_0 = \sqrt{p_0^2c^2 + m^2c^4}$ is the initial kinetic energy of the particle.

Solution We start with the covariant form of the Lorentz force: \[m\frac{dU^\mu}{d\tau} = qF^{\mu\nu}U_\nu.\tag{2}\] Given $U^\mu = (\gamma c, \mathbf{p})$, it becomes \[\frac{d\mathbf{p}}{dt} = q\mathbf{E},\tag{3}\] where $\mathbf{p}$ is the relativistic 3-momentum, namely $\mathbf{p} = \gamma m \dot{\mathbf{x}}$. Thus, we can write \[\frac{d\mathbf{x}}{dt} = \frac{\mathbf{p}}{\gamma m} = \frac{\mathbf{p}c^2}{\mathcal{E}}.\tag{4}\] This gives \[\begin{cases} \dot{x} = \dfrac{p_xc^2}{\sqrt{\mathcal{E}_0^2+p_x^2c^2}},\\ \\ \dot{y} = \dfrac{p_0c^2}{\sqrt{\mathcal{E}_0^2+p_x^2c^2}}. \end{cases} \tag{5}\] Since we can easily solve for $p_x = qEt$, we can solve the two equations and find \[y = \frac{p_0c}{eE}\sinh^{-1}\left(\frac{cqEt}{\mathcal{E}_0}\right),\tag{6}\] and \[x = \frac{c}{qE}\left(\sqrt{\mathcal{E}_0^2+q^2E^2t^2c^2}-\mathcal{E}_0\right).\tag{7}\] Putting these together, we find \[x = \frac{\mathcal{E}_0}{qE}\left(\cosh\!\left(\frac{qEy}{p_0 c}\right)-1\right).\tag{8}\]
