Factorisation Method for the Sech-Squared Potential

Source: D. Tong, Quantum Mechanics, Problem Sheet 1, Question 8.

Sketch the potential

\[U(x) = -\frac{\hbar^2}{m}\operatorname{sech}^2 x\,. \tag{1}\]

Show that the time-independent Schr\"{o}dinger equation for a particle in this potential can be written

\[\hat{A}^\dagger \hat{A}\,\chi = (\mathcal{E}+1)\,\chi\,,\tag{2}\]

where $\mathcal{E} = 2mE/\hbar^2$ and

\[\hat{A} = \frac{d}{d x} + \tanh x\,, \qquad \hat{A}^\dagger = -\frac{d}{d x} + \tanh x\,.\tag{3}\]

Show, by integrating by parts, that for any normalised wavefunction $\chi$,

\[\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \chi^*\hat{A}^\dagger\hat{A}\,\chi\,dx \;=\; \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (\hat{A}\chi)^*(\hat{A}\chi)\,dx\,,\tag{4}\]

and deduce that the eigenvalues of $\hat{A}^\dagger\hat{A}$ are non-negative. Hence show that the ground state (with lowest energy) has $\mathcal{E}\geq -1$. Show that a wavefunction $\chi_0(x)$ is an energy eigenstate with $\mathcal{E}=-1$ iff

\[\frac{d\chi_0}{d x} + \tanh x\,\chi_0 = 0\,.\tag{5}\]

Find and sketch $\chi_0(x)$.

Solution

The Hamiltonian can be written as \[\begin{aligned}H &= -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{d^2}{dx^2} -\frac{\hbar^2}{m}\operatorname{sech}^2 x\\ &= \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\left(-\frac{d^2}{dx^2}-2+\tanh^2x\right)\\ &= \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\left(\frac{d}{dx}+\tanh x\right)\left(-\frac{d}{dx}+\tanh x\right)-\frac{\hbar^2}{m}.\end{aligned}\tag{12}\] Thus, we have \[\hat{A}^\dagger \hat{A}\chi = (\mathcal{E}+1)\chi.\tag{6}\] Rather trivially following from integrating by part, we find \[\int dx \chi^*A^\dagger A\chi = \int dx (A\chi)^*(A\chi),\tag{7}\] justifying that $A^\dagger$ is indeed the Hermitian conjugate to $A$. Given an eigenstate of $A^\dagger A$, $|a\rangle$, with eigenvalue $a$. We have \[\langle a|A^\dagger A|a\rangle = a\langle a |a\rangle = ||A|a\rangle||^2 \geq 0 \implies a\geq0,\tag{8}\] which implies that $\mathcal{E}+1\geq0$. For the ground state with energy $\mathcal{E}=-1$, we have \[\hat{A}^\dagger \hat{A}\chi_0 = 0,\tag{9}\] Taking the inner product of the above with $\chi_0$, we find \[\langle \chi_0|A^\dagger A|\chi_0\rangle = ||A|\chi_0\rangle||^2 = 0 \implies A|\chi_0\rangle=0.\tag{10}\] Solving the above, we find \[\chi_0(x) = \frac{N}{\cosh x},\tag{11}\] where $N$ is a normalisation constant.
