Variational Derivation of Euler's Equation for Inviscid Flow

Source: Cambridge Part II Variational Principles, Problem Sheet 2, Question 9.

The mass density $\rho(t,\mathbf{x})$ and velocity field $\mathbf{v}(t,\mathbf{x})$ of a compressible fluid are constrained by conservation of mass to satisfy the continuity equation \[\dot{\rho} + \nabla\cdot(\rho\mathbf{v}) = 0\,. \tag{$*$} \tag{1}\] Given that the energy density of the fluid is $u(\rho)$, the action (for inviscid irrotational flow) is \[S[\rho,\mathbf{v},\phi] = \int d t\int d^3x\left\{ \tfrac{1}{2}\rho|\mathbf{v}|^2 - u(\rho) + \phi\left[\dot{\rho}+\nabla\cdot(\rho\mathbf{v})\right] \right\},\tag{2}\] where $\phi(t,\mathbf{x})$ is a Lagrange multiplier field imposing the continuity condition (1). Find the Euler--Lagrange equations for this action. Show that they imply $\mathbf{v}=\nabla\phi$ (so $\phi$ is the velocity potential). Given that the fluid pressure $P(t,\mathbf{x})$ satisfies \[\nabla P = \rho\nabla h(t,\mathbf{x})\,, \qquad h = u'(\rho)\,,\tag{3}\] deduce Euler's equation for inviscid irrotational flow: \[\rho\left[\dot{\mathbf{v}} + (\mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla)\mathbf{v}\right] = -\nabla P\,.\tag{4}\]

Solution First, we consider the equation of motion of $\mathbf{v}$: \[\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial v_i} = -\rho v_i + \phi(\partial_i \rho), \quad \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial (\partial_i v_j )} = \rho \phi \delta_{ij} \implies \nabla\phi = \mathbf{v}.\tag{5}\] Then, for the EoM with respect to $\rho$, we find \[\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \rho} = -\frac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2 - u'(\rho), \quad \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \dot{\rho}} = \phi \implies \dot{\phi} = -\frac{1}{2}|\mathbf{v}|^2 - u'(\rho).\tag{6}\] Finally, the constraint is \[\dot{\rho} + \nabla\cdot(\rho\mathbf{v}) = 0.\tag{7}\]

Putting them together, one can easily find \[\rho\left[\dot{\mathbf{v}} + (\mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla)\mathbf{v}\right] = -\nabla P.\tag{8}\]
